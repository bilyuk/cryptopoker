# Legal review gate and allow-list launch

Public launch of Blockchain-Backed Rooms is gated on specialized gambling-law review and jurisdiction controls. Non-custodial escrow reduces custody and money-transmission risk, but it does not make real-money online poker unregulated; the launch posture should be jurisdiction allow-listing rather than block-listing, with high-risk jurisdictions unavailable by default until counsel approves them.

The first blockchain-backed version will not take rake: no percentage of pots, hands, Buy-Ins, Top-Ups, or Checkouts. Revenue, if any, must be no-take, subscription, or flat per-Room fees until a separate legal review approves any gameplay-linked monetization. Rake is treated as a separate product and licensing decision because it strongly signals operating a poker room for profit.

A closed, invite-only alpha may run during legal review in known-safe jurisdictions with explicit participant screening, age/legal-location self-attestation, and geo controls. Public launch requires a written legal position on jurisdiction strategy, marketing language, player and host disclosures, AML and sanctions posture, tax-reporting implications, and the Host-as-operator question created by Host-Arbitrated Payouts.
