# Compliance scope for non-custodial v1

The first blockchain-backed version will stay narrowly scoped to private poker coordination plus non-custodial USDC escrow. Players must arrive with native USDC on Base in their own wallets; the product will not provide fiat on-ramps, fiat off-ramps, token swaps, platform-held balances, optional KYC collection, promotional tokens, or loyalty credits.

This scope avoids turning v1 into a wallet, exchange, money transmitter, or fiat payments product, but it does not remove baseline compliance work. V1 still needs jurisdiction blocking, sanctions and wallet-risk screening, age attestation, clear terms explaining that the platform does not custody funds or maintain balances, and user-responsibility disclosures for wallet security, taxes, and local legality.

Educational guidance about getting native USDC on Base should remain generic. The product should avoid direct exchange integrations, affiliate purchase flows, or "buy USDC here" actions until a separate legal review and product decision explicitly expands the compliance scope. Gambling-law licensing and jurisdiction risk are separate from financial-services scope and must be decided before public launch.
